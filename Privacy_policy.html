About this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Star Medical Centre (“we” or “us”) collects, uses, and shares information about you when you use the websites and mobile application provided by Star Medical Centre

Notice

This Privacy Policy pertains to both personally identifiable information and healthcare-related information (also known as Protected Health Information or PHI) that you provide to us for the purpose of receiving medical care through our Services. These types of information are subject to the laws and regulations of the UAE, including both the UAE Federal laws and those of specific organizations such as the Department of Health Abu Dhabi and the UAE Ministry of Health.

The UAE Laws dictate how Star Medical Centre can use and disclose your personally identifiable information and PHI, as well as outlining your rights with regards to this information. This Privacy Policy complements these laws and regulations. In case of any conflict between this Privacy Policy and UAE Laws, Department of Health Abu Dhabi, UAE Ministry of Health regulations, the UAE Laws will take precedence.

Information We Collect

Star Medical Centre has the potential to gather several types of information, including

  • contact details like your full name, email address, mobile number, and physical address
  • personal health details such as your medical history, diagnosis, and health insurance
  • doctor reviews, and any other data you agree to provide

We may merge this information with data we already obtained about you. If there are any errors in your personally identifiable information, e.g., name, mobile number, date of birth, etc., we will make corrections to ensure accuracy, and with your approval, we can alter or update your personal information.

When you use our Services, we may automatically collect specific information, such as your computer’s Internet Protocol (IP) address, device and advertising identifiers, browser type, operating system, internet service provider, pages visited before and after using our Services, the date and time of your visit, and other standard server log information, as well as information about the links clicked and pages viewed within the Services.

We may use technologies such as cookies, pixel tags, and Local Shared Objects to automatically gather this information. Cookies are tiny bits of data stored by your computer’s web browser, while pixel tags are small images or data pieces embedded in images that can recognize cookies, the time and date a page is viewed, description of the page where the pixel tag is placed, and other details about your computer or device. Local Shared Objects, also known as “Flash Cookies,” are comparable to regular cookies, except that they can be larger and are downloaded to a computer or mobile device by the Adobe Flash media player. By using our Services, you are giving us consent to use cookies and similar technologies.

We may also collect technical data to resolve technical problems and enhance our Services. Your device or browser settings may allow you to control the collection of this technical data. By using our Services, you are consenting to us, or any party acting on our behalf, collecting this technical data.

Information we obtain from your healthcare providers and other sources.

In connection with Services that involve medical treatment, we may collect medical records from your past, current, and future health care providers (to the extent applicable). This may include information about your diagnosis, previous treatments, general health, laboratory and pathology test results and reports, social histories, any family history of illness, and records about phone calls and emails related to your illness.

We may also receive information about you from other sources, including through third-party services and organizations. We may combine our first-party data, such as your email address or name, with third-party data from other sources and use this to contact you (e.g. through direct mail). For example, if you access third-party services, such as Facebook, Google, or Twitter, through the Services to login to the Services or to share information about your experience on the Services with others, we may collect information from these third-party services.

Information from Third-Party Services

If you access the Services from an advertisement on a third-party website, application, or other service (a “Third-Party Service”) we may receive information from the owner of the Third-Party Service related to you or that advertisement.
